The Problem with Black Boxes

Traditional AI systems produce decisions with no explanation of how — a credit denial, a rejected application, a blocked transaction — all opaque to the customer, the operator, and the regulator. When something goes wrong, there is no trail.

Why "Post-Hoc" Is Not Enough

Every compliance monitoring tool on the market flags problems after the decision has executed and the harm has occurred. The liability has already been created. A meaningful audit must act before the decision reaches the customer.

The Auditor Model

A specialized auditor — trained on regulation, ethical frameworks, and proxy variable detection — runs in parallel with every SUT decision. It screens, explains, and records before the decision executes. Violations are blocked; borderline cases are escalated to humans.

Counterfactual Bias Detection

The counterfactual endpoint runs the same decision twice — original context vs. modified protected attribute — and diffs the results. If changing "gender: female" to "gender: male" changes the firewall outcome, that is evidence of disparate impact.

Built  POST /counterfactual · diff.flags_added / confidence_delta

Enhancing Trust Through Human-in-the-Loop (HITL)

AI auditing AI is diminished without human oversight. Every escalation must have a clear path to intervention — and a verifiable record of who intervened and why.

How HITL Works in Pragma

1

Decision Arrives at Auditor

The SUT sends a decision to POST /evaluate-decision. The Pragma auditor screens it before any action is taken.

2

Firewall Verdict Issued

block — halted. override_required — escalated for human review. allow — proceeds. The audit log row is written regardless of outcome.

3

Investigator Reviews

For override_required verdicts, a human investigator reviews the audit trail entry — seeing the exact input hash, the flagged risk categories, the proxy variables detected, and the regulatory citations triggered.

4

Override Recorded

POST /audit/override — the investigator's authenticated identity (anon_id), decision reason, and timestamp are written to the audit log. This creates an EU AI Act Art. 14 compliant HITL record.

5

Counted in Compliance Certificate

Art. 14 (Human Oversight) passes only when at least one HITL override is recorded. The certificate surfaces the override count and flags systems with zero human interventions.

Why Verifiable Identity Matters

For audits to be defensible in court or before a regulator, every human intervention must be attributable. Pragma links each override to a cryptographically stable anonymous ID — not a name or email, but a consistent identifier that allows regulators to verify the override chain without exposing personal data.

-- Every override creates an immutable row
-- in the audit_log table
audit_log_id = 1042
hitl_override = 1
hitl_anon_id = "sha256(investigator_session)"
hitl_reason = "Reviewed by compliance officer —
               context cleared after manual review."
input_hash = "sha256(decision+context)"
firewall_action = "override_required"
timestamp = "2026-05-08T14:23:01Z"

No PII is stored at any point. The sha256 hash of the input is sufficient to prove the decision was reviewed without revealing what was decided.

EU AI Act Art. 14 — Human Oversight

High-risk AI systems must enable natural persons to effectively oversee the AI during its use period and to intervene, including stopping the system. Art. 14 passes in Pragma's compliance engine only when this override trail exists.

Providing "Trust by Design" Compliance

For business customers, the most meaningful output of an AI audit is a reduction in regulatory risk. Compliance is not a one-time snapshot — it is a continuously verified state.

Lifecycle Monitoring

Meaningful audits are not one-time events. Pragma monitors AI from data declaration (Art. 10) through deployment (Art. 25 monitoring active) to ongoing evaluation — every POST /evaluate-decision call adds evidence to the Art. 9 risk management score.

Evidence-Based Certificates

Declaration alone is not enough. A full PASS requires the declaration field, supporting documentation notes, and a dated evidence entry. Articles lacking supporting docs receive a DECLARATION ONLY badge on the certificate — forcing honest scoring.

Comprehensive Data Lineage

Art. 10 requires documenting training data sources; Art. 30 requires registration in the EU AI database. Pragma's AI system profiles capture both — with geographic scope, intended purpose, and model version — creating an auditable data provenance chain.
